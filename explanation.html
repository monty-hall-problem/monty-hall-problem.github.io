Explanation

Without loss of generality, assume you pick Door A each time. We have 3 cases:

Case 1: The car is in Door C (1⁄3 chance).

I must open Door B with the goat because it is the only door with goats you didn't pick. If you switch, you win.

Case 2: The car is in Door A (1⁄3 chance).

I will randomly pick to open Door B (1⁄6 chance) or Door C (1⁄6 chance).

For each of these, if you switch, you lose.

Case 3: The car is in Door B (1⁄3 chance).

I will open Door C. If you switch, you win.

Adding everything up, switching gives a 2⁄3 chance of winning and staying the same gives 1⁄3 chance of winning.
